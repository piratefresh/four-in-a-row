# Word Poker: River Run Power-Ups

## Power-Up Design Rule

Power-ups should be simple.

Best format:

```text
Condition -> Reward
```

Examples:

- If your word ends in S, gain +5 points.
- If your word has 3 or more vowels, gain +8 points.
- If you use all revealed tiles, gain +10 points.
- Your first Flip each hand costs 1 less.

---

## Power-Up Categories

Power-ups are grouped into 5 categories:

1. Scoring Power-Ups
2. Flip Power-Ups
3. Economy Power-Ups
4. Comeback Power-Ups
5. Phase Power-Ups

---

Examples:

- Loaded Flip

- Coupon

---

## Legendary

Big, weird power-ups that change the hand.

Examples:

### Ghost Tile

Add a blank tile once per hand.

---

### Double River

Your River word scores twice, but Deal and Turn score half.

---

### Mirror Word

If your word starts with the same letter as your opponent's word, gain +20 points.

# Balance Notes

Power-ups should create strategy, not decide the game by themselves.

Good power-ups:

- Encourage different word choices.
- Are easy to understand.
- Create visible moments.
- Have clear conditions.
- Do not remove the opponent's ability to play.

Avoid early:

- Random huge bonuses.
- Power-ups that invalidate opponent words.
- Permanent attacks.
- Effects with too much text.
- Anything that requires a player to read a paragraph during a 20-second shop.
